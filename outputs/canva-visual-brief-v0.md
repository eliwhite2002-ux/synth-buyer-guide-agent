# Canva Visual Brief — First-Time Buyer Guide v0

## Brand direction

Serious, practical, owner-experience oriented. No explicit imagery. No fake product photos. Use neutral icons, product-card mockups, checklists, warning cards, storage/cleaning workflow motifs, and dark editorial styling.

## Asset 1 — Article cover

Format: 16:9 article cover.

Headline: First-Time Sex Doll Buyer Guide

Subhead: How to buy without regret.

Visual direction: dark editorial background, checklist/product-card motif, subtle weight/storage icons, no explicit imagery.

## Asset 2 — Five mistakes carousel

Format: 5 square cards.

Card 1: Buying too much doll.

Card 2: Ignoring weight.

Card 3: Choosing material blindly.

Card 4: Trusting photos.

Card 5: No private logistics plan.

Visual direction: one icon per card: box/scale/drop/camera/closet. Keep it simple and readable.

## Asset 3 — Real-home test checklist

Format: vertical infographic.

Questions:

- Can you move it?
- Can you clean it?
- Can it dry properly?
- Can you store it?
- Can you receive it privately?
- Will you still maintain it after the novelty wears off?

Visual direction: apartment/closet/bathroom workflow. No product nudity.

## Asset 4 — Weight warning card

Format: article insert and short-video background.

Copy: Weight is not a footnote. It decides whether you actually use the product.

Visual direction: scale/handling motif, warning-card style.

## Asset 5 — Buyer decision table graphic

Format: wide article image.

Columns:

- Buyer situation.
- Better first direction.
- Avoid first.

Visual direction: practical buying tool, like a clean product decision table.

## Asset 6 — Product card template

Format: reusable card layout.

Fields:

- Vendor.
- Product.
- Material.
- Height.
- Weight.
- Price.
- Image rights.
- Best for.
- Avoid if.
- Owner advantage.
- Owner risk.
- Status: Include / Watch / Reject / Needs Review.

Visual direction: modular product-card frame that can be reused when product extraction is added.
